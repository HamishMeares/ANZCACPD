Tutorial (very short):
  • Risk driver: management is mainly determined by device type and whether the patient is pacemaker-dependent, plus the predicted EMI burden.
  • High EMI + dependency: prefer planned asynchronous pacing via programming (VOO/DOO), rather than assuming magnet behaviour.
  • Magnet behaviour varies: response differs by manufacturer/model, and for some systems magnet may be absent or non-sustained.
  • Avoid unnecessary asynchronous pacing: in patients with strong intrinsic rhythm, asynchronous pacing can create R-on-T risk.
  • Diathermy pad placement: aim to keep current path away from generator/leads—often contralateral limb placement.
Note: Always align with your local CIED guideline/device clinic advice for your site.
